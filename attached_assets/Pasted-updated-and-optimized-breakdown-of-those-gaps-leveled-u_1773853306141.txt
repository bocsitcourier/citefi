If you must poll, do it intelligently.The Optimization: Implement the Page Visibility API. If the tab isn't active, clearInterval.AudioContext Leak: Ensure you are calling audioContext.close() on unmount. Enterprise apps often trigger "Auto-play" blocks; wrap your audio init in a user-interaction listener to prevent "suspended" state leaks.Platinum Move: Suggest moving from Polling to Server-Sent Events (SSE). It’s a single long-lived HTTP connection that is significantly lighter on the server than constant 5s pings.Fix 2 & 3: Video Strategy — Stop the "Ghost" DownloadsEven preload="metadata" can trigger a 2MB+ "sniff" of the file. On a page with 10 videos, that’s 20MB of wasted data on load.The Optimization: Use preload="none".The "Platinum" Addition: Use an Intersection Observer. Only switch preload to metadata when the video is scrolled into the viewport.Posters: Use Next.js Image Optimization for the poster attribute. Don't just point to a raw S3 URL; serve a WebP/AVIF version to save LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) time.Fix 4: Public Objects API — Media Streaming InfrastructureWithout Range requests, users on iOS/Safari literally cannot seek through your videos—they will just reset to the beginning.The Optimization: * ETag: Use strong ETags (hash-based) to prevent re-downloading unchanged 10MB video files.Cache-Control: public, max-age=31536000, immutable.Range Support: Ensure your backend (or Cloud Storage) is configured for Accept-Ranges: bytes. This allows the browser to request "chunks" (e.g., just the middle 5 seconds of a video).🛠 State & Infrastructure ManagementFix 5: queryClient — Intelligent CachingReact Query defaults are often too aggressive for enterprise apps, leading to "Refetch Storms."The Optimization: * staleTime: Set to at least 1000 * 60 * 5 (5 minutes) for data that doesn't change often.gcTime (formerly cacheTime): Should be longer than staleTime.Platinum Move: Enable refetchOnWindowFocus: false for the Media/Social pages.
