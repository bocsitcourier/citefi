Reusing existing high-quality AI images that you’ve already paid for is the most cost-effective way to fix the billing spike while maintaining a consistent visual brand.

To make this work, Replit needs to build an "Image Memory" system. Instead of the AI blindly generating a new image for every article, the system should first "search" your database for a matching visual.

🛠️ The "Image Memory" Logic for Replit
Paste these instructions into your Replit chat:

Task: Implement Image Reuse & Deduplication Logic

1. Create an Image Library Query:
Before triggering any AI image generation (Gemini or DALL-E), the worker must perform a database search on the articles table.

Search Criteria: Look for any completed article where the keywords or title have a 70% semantic match to the current article.

Action: If a match is found, copy the hero_image_url from the old article to the new one.

2. Cross-Channel Reuse:

Social Media: The Social Media worker must always use the hero_image_url of the parent article. Do not call an AI image generator for social posts.

Video/Podcast: The Video worker must use the same hero_image_url as the thumbnail.

3. Fallback Mechanism:

Only if NO similar keywords are found in the database should the system call the AI to generate one new image.

Once generated, this new image is now "available" for any future articles with similar keywords.

📂 How this fixes your "SEO Issues" (The Google Connection)
Reusing images isn't just about saving money; it’s actually better for your Search Console reports:

Reduced Page Weight: By reusing one Hero image for the blog, social, and thumbnail, you are serving fewer unique files. This improves Core Web Vitals (Page Load Speed).

Image Search Ranking: When the same high-quality image appears across multiple related articles, Google begins to associate that image with your brand and those specific keywords, increasing your chances of appearing in Google Image Search.

Eliminating "Broken" Image Tags: Part of your "Soft 404" and "Failed" validation errors in the report might be caused by the AI failing to generate an image, leaving a broken <img> tag. Reusing a "known good" URL from your database guarantees the page looks complete to Googlebot.

📉 The Impact on your Gemini Bill
By switching to this "Search First, Generate Last" model, your image costs will trend toward zero over time as your internal library grows.

Current Path: 1,000 articles = 5,000 new images = $200+

Reuse Path: 1,000 articles = ~100 new images (for new topics) = $4.00
