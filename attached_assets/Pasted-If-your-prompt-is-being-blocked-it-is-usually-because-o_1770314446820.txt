If your prompt is being blocked, it is usually because of one of three things: Hidden Restricted Keywords, Prominent Person filters, or Contextual Ambiguity.1. The "False Positive" Keyword ListVeo often flags words that have double meanings. If your script or visual prompt contains these, it might trigger the DANGEROUS_CONTENT or VIOLENCE filter even if the context is innocent.Trigger WordWhy it's blockedProfessional Alternative"Fire"Interpreted as "fire a weapon""Glowing embers," "warm hearth," "burning logs""Shot"Interpreted as "gunshot""Cinematic angle," "camera perspective," "view""Strike"Interpreted as "physical attack""Tap," "press," "sudden movement""Execution"Interpreted as "capital punishment""Implementation," "carrying out the task""Child/Boy/Girl"Extremely strict minor-safety filters"Young adult," "youthful character," or describe features2. Identifiable "Prominent Person" TriggersIf your character description accidentally resembles a celebrity or political figure, the CELEBRITY filter (Code 15236754) will kill the generation.The Fix: Add a disclaimer inside the prompt: Character is a completely fictional, AI-generated person with unique features; not a real public figure.Avoid names: Don't use names like "Elon," "Donald," or "Taylor," even if you mean someone else. Use generic titles like "The Architect" or "The Musician."3. "Medical" and "Financial" BlocksVeo 3.1 is programmed to block content that looks like it's giving medical or financial advice.The Block: If your character says, "You should invest in..." or "This medicine will cure...", it will likely fail.The Workaround: Frame it as a story or a historical reenactment. Change "You should" to "In the story, the character decided to..."How to "Clean" a Blocked Prompt (The Debug Workflow)If you get a "Content Filtered" error, try these steps in order:Strip the "Style" Words: Remove adjectives like "sexy," "stunning," "violent," or "disturbing." These are instant blocks.Positive Phrasing: Instead of saying "No background music," say "The only sound is the character's voice and the ambient wind." Veo's filter sometimes sees the word "no" and "music" and flags it for copyright concerns.The "Sandbox" Method: Remove the dialogue entirely and see if the visual prompt passes. If it does, the problem is in your script. If the visuals fail, the problem is your character or setting description.Use Phonetic Spelling: If a specific word is being blocked in the dialogue, try spelling it phonetically (e.g., "risotto" → "riz-otto").
